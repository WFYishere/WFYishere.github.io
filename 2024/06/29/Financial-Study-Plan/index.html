Finance Study Plan

Posted on 2024-06-29 In Documentaries Word count in article: 4.6k Reading time ≈ 4 mins.

Learn to be a pro in finance in 200 days (spend 40 min each day!).

Introduction

Check out more information on the study plan and how I followed the plan HERE

Plan for Learning Finance

Week 1-4: Introduction to Finance and Economics

Week 1-2: Basics of Finance

  • Day 1-3: Read "The Richest Man in Babylon" by George S. Clason.

  • Day 4-7: Read "Rich Dad Poor Dad" by Robert T. Kiyosaki.

  • Day 8-14: Enroll in an online course on Coursera or Khan Academy covering the basics of finance. Focus on understanding terms like assets, liabilities, equity, revenue, expenses, and net income.

Week 3-4: Introduction to Economics

  • Day 15-18: Read "Economics in One Lesson" by Henry Hazlitt.

  • Day 19-28: Take an introductory course on economics from Coursera or Khan Academy. Focus on supply and demand, market structures, and economic indicators.

Week 5-8: Personal Finance and Financial Statements

Week 5-6: Personal Finance

  • Day 29-32: Read "Your Money or Your Life" by Vicki Robin and Joe Dominguez.

  • Day 33-42: Complete a personal finance course on Coursera or Khan Academy. Learn about budgeting, saving, debt management, and retirement planning.

Week 7-8: Financial Statements

  • Day 43-45: Read "The Intelligent Investor" by Benjamin Graham (focus on the early chapters).

  • Day 46-56: Study financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement) through an online course or tutorial.

Week 9-12: Stock Market and Investment Basics

Week 9-10: Introduction to the Stock Market

  • Day 57-60: Read "A Random Walk Down Wall Street" by Burton G. Malkiel.

  • Day 61-70: Take an introductory course on the stock market from Coursera or Khan Academy. Learn about stocks, bonds, mutual funds, ETFs, and how the stock market works.

Week 11-12: Investment Strategies

  • Day 71-73: Read "The Little Book of Common Sense Investing" by John C. Bogle.

  • Day 74-84: Study different investment strategies (value investing, growth investing, dividend investing) through online resources or courses.

Week 13-16: Stock Analysis and Practice

Week 13-14: Stock Analysis

  • Day 85-87: Read "One Up On Wall Street" by Peter Lynch.

  • Day 88-98: Learn about fundamental analysis (analyzing financial statements, ratios) and technical analysis (charts, patterns) through online courses or tutorials.

Week 15-16: Practice and Simulations

  • Day 99-101: Use a stock market simulator (like Investopedia's Stock Simulator) to practice buying and selling stocks.

  • Day 102-112: Continue practicing with the simulator, focusing on applying the concepts of fundamental and technical analysis.

Week 17-20: Advanced Topics in Investment and Economics

Week 17-18: Advanced Investment Concepts

  • Day 113-115: Read "Security Analysis" by Benjamin Graham and David Dodd.

  • Day 116-126: Study advanced topics like portfolio management, asset allocation, and risk management through online courses or books.

Week 19: Behavioral Finance

  • Day 127-129: Read "Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman.

  • Day 130-140: Learn about behavioral finance concepts through online courses or articles. Understand how psychology affects investment decisions.

Week 20: Macroeconomics

  • Day 141-143: Read "Principles of Economics" by N. Gregory Mankiw (focus on macroeconomics sections).

  • Day 144-154: Take a course on macroeconomics. Focus on topics like GDP, inflation, unemployment, monetary policy, and fiscal policy.

Week 21-24: Microeconomics and Practical Application

Week 21: Microeconomics

  • Day 155-157: Read "Principles of Economics" by N. Gregory Mankiw (focus on microeconomics sections).

  • Day 158-168: Take a course on microeconomics. Focus on topics like consumer behavior, market structures, and game theory.

Week 22-23: Financial Modeling

  • Day 169-172: Learn the basics of financial modeling through online courses or tutorials. Understand how to build financial models in Excel.

  • Day 173-182: Practice creating simple financial models for companies.

Week 24-25: Investment Analysis

  • Day 183-185: Study case studies of successful investors and their strategies.

  • Day 186-195: Analyze real-world companies using the skills you've learned. Write reports on your findings.

Week 26-28: Economics in Practice and Capstone Project

Week 26: Economics in Practice

  • Day 196-198: Read "Freakonomics" by Steven D. Levitt and Stephen J. Dubner.

  • Day 199-202: Study real-world economic events and their impacts through articles, documentaries, and case studies.

Week 27-28: Capstone Project

  • Day 203-209: Choose a topic of interest (e.g., a specific company, an economic event, an investment strategy) and conduct an in-depth analysis.

  • Day 210-215: Compile your findings into a report or presentation.

  • Day 216-220: Review your study plan, identify areas for further study, and set goals for continued learning.

Additional Resources

  • Books:

    • "The Essays of Warren Buffett" by Warren Buffett

    • "Fooled by Randomness" by Nassim Nicholas Taleb

    • "Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits" by Philip Fisher

  • Websites and Blogs:

    • Investopedia

    • The Motley Fool

    • Seeking Alpha

  • Podcasts:

    • "Planet Money" by NPR

    • "The Investors Podcast"

    • "We Study Billionaires"

  • YouTube Channels:

    • Khan Academy (Finance and Capital Markets)

    • Graham Stephan

    • The Plain Bagel

Final Tips

  • Consistency: Dedicate time daily to studying and practice.

  • Networking: Join finance and investment clubs or online communities.

  • Practice: Regularly apply what you learn through simulations and real-world analysis.

Good luck with your studies!
